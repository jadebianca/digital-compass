Checking Your Privacy Settings

By: Mallari, Vince Xyron A. • April 9, 2026

Intro • App Permissions and Access • Security Settings and Authentication • Account Visibility • Managing Personal Information • Conclusion

Today, almost everything that we do involves the collection of our personal data. Things like social media, shopping online or even just browsing the internet collects information from us in order to improve the services, personalize experience and many more. However, the data that was gathered can be sold to third parties which then leads to privacy risks if not managed. This is why checking your privacy settings is important as it protects your personal information.

App Permissions and Access

A lot of applications usually requests access to informations such as location, camera, contacts and microphone. Some permissions are necessary in order to function properly, others many not be as essential. Reviewing the app permissions allows you to control the data that is being collected. An example of this is social media platforms like Instagram, Tiktok and Facebook they allow you to decide on who can see your posts and can interact with it. It is important to regularly check these permissions and disable the ones that are unnecessary This helps reduce the risk of unauthorized access to your personal data. It also ensures that your information is only shared with features that you trust and actually use.

Security Settings and Authentication

Strengthening your account security is essential in protecting your personal data. One effective way to do this is by enabling two-factor authentication (2FA), which adds an extra layer of protection by requiring a second verification step, such as a code sent to your device. Additionally, using strong and unique passwords for each account helps prevent unauthorized access. Password managers can assist in securely storing and generating complex passwords, making it easier to maintain strong security across multiple accounts.

Account Visibility

Account visibility settings determine who can see your profile, posts, and personal information online. Many platforms allow you to switch between public and private accounts, giving you control over your audience. By limiting visibility to trusted individuals, you can reduce the risk of exposing sensitive information. It is also important to review settings related to comments, tags, and shared content to ensure that only appropriate people can interact with your account.

Managing Personal Information

Managing your personal information involves being mindful of what you share online and how it is used. Avoid providing unnecessary details such as your exact location, contact information, or sensitive data unless required. You should also consider turning off personalized ads, as these often rely on tracking your online behavior and preferences. Regularly updating your device software and applications helps protect against security vulnerabilities, ensuring that your information remains secure from potential threats.

Conclusion

In a digital world where personal data is constantly being collected, taking control of your privacy settings is more important than ever. By reviewing app permissions, strengthening security measures, limiting account visibility, and managing the information you share, you can significantly reduce privacy risks. Staying informed and proactive ensures that your personal data remains protected. Ultimately, being proactive about privacy empowers you to enjoy online services while maintaining control over your digital identity.
