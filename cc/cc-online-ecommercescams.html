E-commerce Scams

By: Hamoy, Kurt Tobey • April 2026

Intro • What Are E-commerce Scams • Common Scam Types • How They Operate • Warning Signs • Shopping Safely Online • Conclusion

Introduction

Online shopping has transformed the way Filipinos buy and sell goods, but it has also created new opportunities for fraud. E-commerce scams are deceptive schemes carried out on digital shopping platforms, social media marketplaces, or fake websites to steal money or personal information from buyers and sellers. With millions of Filipinos using platforms like Shopee, Lazada, and Facebook Marketplace, the risk of encountering a scam is higher than ever. The Department of Trade and Industry (DTI) receives thousands of online shopping complaints every year. Understanding e-commerce scams is essential for every digital shopper in the Philippines.

What Are E-commerce Scams?

E-commerce scams involve fraudulent transactions in online buying and selling, where either the buyer or seller deceives the other party for financial gain. These scams can take many forms, from fake product listings to fraudulent payment schemes. The DTI and PNP-ACG have recorded a significant increase in e-commerce fraud complaints, particularly during holidays and sale seasons when online shopping activity peaks. Victims often feel helpless after being scammed, especially when transactions happen outside official platforms where buyer protection is minimal. Sellers can also be victims, as fraudulent buyers may use fake payment confirmations or file false refund claims. Philippine law, specifically RA 10175 and the Consumer Act, provides legal recourse for victims of online shopping fraud. However, the best protection is always prevention through awareness and smart shopping habits.

Common Types of E-commerce Scams

There are numerous types of e-commerce scams, each exploiting different aspects of online transactions. Non-delivery scams occur when a buyer pays for an item that is never shipped. Counterfeit goods scams involve selling fake or substandard products that are advertised as genuine. Overpayment scams target sellers, where a fake buyer sends more than the agreed price and asks for a refund of the excess — often using a fraudulent check or payment. Fake websites mimic legitimate online stores to steal payment information. Return fraud involves sending back a damaged or completely different item after receiving a genuine product. Phishing via shopping platforms uses fake order confirmation emails to steal login credentials. Social media marketplace scams operate through Facebook and Instagram, where sellers disappear after receiving payment.

How E-commerce Scammers Operate

E-commerce scammers are skilled at building false credibility to gain the trust of their victims before executing the fraud. Fake sellers often use stolen product photos and create listings with extremely attractive prices to lure bargain hunters. They may have fabricated reviews and ratings to appear legitimate. Scammers typically insist on payment methods outside the platform — such as bank transfers or GCash — to avoid platform protections and make funds harder to trace. Once the payment is received, the scammer blocks the buyer and disappears. Some scammers use social media ads that lead to professional-looking fake websites with fake customer service contacts. Others target sellers by pretending to place large orders and sending fake payment screenshots. The use of urgency and scarcity tactics — such as "only 2 left at this price" — pressures buyers into acting quickly without verifying the seller.

Warning Signs of an Online Shopping Scam

There are several tell-tale signs that an online listing or seller may not be legitimate. Be suspicious of prices that are dramatically lower than what is found on official brand websites or other established stores — if it seems too good to be true, it probably is. Sellers who insist on payment outside the platform or who refuse to use cash-on-delivery should be treated with extreme caution. New seller accounts with no reviews, few followers, or a suspiciously perfect rating with only generic-sounding feedback are red flags. Poor product photos that look heavily edited or are clearly taken from the internet suggest the seller may not actually have the product. Be wary of sellers who are difficult to contact or who give vague answers about shipping timelines and return policies. Fake websites often have no physical address, no working customer service number, and use generic or misspelled domain names. Trust your instincts — if something feels off, it probably is.

How to Shop Online Safely

Safe online shopping requires a combination of careful verification, use of platform protections, and secure payment methods. Always buy from verified sellers on established platforms like Shopee or Lazada, which offer buyer protection and dispute resolution services. Avoid making payments outside the platform, especially through bank transfers to personal accounts. Use credit cards or payment services that offer fraud protection when making online purchases. Take screenshots of product listings, chat conversations, and payment confirmations as evidence in case a dispute arises. Read reviews carefully and check the seller's overall rating and transaction history before buying. For large purchases, consider using cash-on-delivery as a safer option. If you believe you have been scammed, report it to the DTI, PNP-ACG, or the platform where the transaction took place immediately.

Conclusion

E-commerce scams are a growing problem that affects millions of online shoppers in the Philippines every year. From non-delivery and fake websites to overpayment and counterfeit goods scams, fraudsters have found many ways to exploit online shopping platforms. Knowing the warning signs and understanding how these scams operate is the best protection a consumer can have. Shopping on legitimate, buyer-protected platforms and avoiding off-platform payments significantly reduces your risk. When in doubt about a seller or a deal, take the time to verify before making any payment. Report suspicious sellers and listings to protect not just yourself but other shoppers as well. Safe online shopping is possible — it just requires a little more vigilance.
